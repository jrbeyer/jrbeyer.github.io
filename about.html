About Me

My name is Jacob, and I am a computer engineer interested in embedded computing and controls design. Most recently I was a software engineering intern for General Motors developing a test automation environment for next-generation engine control modules utilizing hardware-in-the-loop, but in previous research and work I have engaged with robotics, controls engineering, low- and high-voltage electrical hardware, and register-transfer level design.

I am a current Master of Engineering student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Princeton University. I also completed my undergraduate studies at Princeton, graduating Highest Honors with a bachelor's degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering, along with Phi Beta Kappa, Tau Beta Pi, and Sigma Xi nominations. My research and academics earned me the Bradley Dickinson Award for System Design from my department, as well as the James Hayes-Edgar Palmer Prize in Engineering from the School of Engineering and Applied Science.

In my free time, I love to make music and explore the outdoors. I play the French horn and guitar regularly, and I go backpacking whenever I can. My favorite trips have included Glacier, Zion, Death Valley, and Pictured Rocks.
